### Books

### Essays

I read some very basic essays on a site known as lesswrong regarding [power seeking agents](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/CtnHpECuoq6eLL8fu/power-seeking-agents-will-likely-be-developed) and [simulators](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vJFdjigzmcXMhNTsx/simulators). 

In conjunction with reading Cixous, I wonder what we'll think about the Self and the Other if we do really reach a scenario where there is a convergent "artificial self." In maybe a Lacanian sense, aren't we the mother of AI, in a way? Will such an AI ever realize that we are an incomplete Other? But whatever, I'm not a Lacan scholar nor an AI researcher.

### Economic reading of the week
 
I read [this article](https://voxdev.org/topic/methods-measurement/what-1-day-global-poverty-line-gets-wrong) from Lant Pritchett in VoxDev about the dollar-a-day global poverty line. I mostly agree--it is important to recognize that the next rung up from extreme poverty is still, well, poverty. Hence it is necessary to shift development institutions back towards broader policies of economic development if we wish to lift people out of poverty point blank, not just extreme poverty!
